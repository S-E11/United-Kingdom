Descriptions of United Kingdom's Top Sights

British Museum

British Museum is a national museum with particularly outstanding holdings in archaeology and ethnography. It is located in the Bloomsbury district of the borough of Camden, and was established in 1753. Its famous round Reading Room was built in the 1850s. Its most famous holdings are the Elgin Marbles, consisting mainly of architectural details from the Parthenon at Athens; other Greek sculptures from the Mausoleum of Halicarnassus and from the Temple of Artemis at Ephesus; the Rosetta Stone from Egypt.

Stonehenge

Stonehenge is a prehistoric stone circle monument located on Salisbury Plain, about 8 miles (13 km) north of Salisbury, Wiltshire, England. It was built in six stages between 3000 and 1520 BC and was designated a UNESCO World Heritage site. One entrance faces the sunrise. It consists of 2 circles of huge standing stones.

The National Gallery

National Gallery, art museum in London that houses Great Britain’s national collection of European paintings. It is located on the north side of Trafalgar Square, Westminster. The collection now comprises some 2,000 works, and has the most comprehensive collection of Italian Renaissance paintings outside Italy.
